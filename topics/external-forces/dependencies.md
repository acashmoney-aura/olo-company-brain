# Olo external forces and dependencies

## Key dependency map

Olo is not just selling software features. It is operating inside a dependency web.

### 1. Restaurant digital order volume
If restaurant brands push harder on direct digital ordering, Olo likely benefits. If that volume shifts back toward marketplaces or stalls, Olo's growth logic weakens.

### 2. POS and integration quality
Because Olo markets an open platform and large integration network, integration depth and reliability are strategic assets, not implementation details.

### 3. Delivery marketplace strategy
Dispatch exists partly because marketplaces are simultaneously distribution partners and strategic threats.

### 4. Payment economics and fraud pressure
Payments can deepen monetization but also create exposure to fraud, authorization, and processor economics.

### 5. Restaurant operating budgets
Olo ultimately sells into a sector with real labor, margin, and capex constraints.

### 6. Post-acquisition ownership
Thoma Bravo ownership may accelerate investment and operational discipline, but it also reduces public transparency.
