# Olo business overview

## High-confidence description

Olo is best understood as restaurant digital-commerce infrastructure for restaurant brands, especially multi-unit or enterprise chains that want to own direct ordering, payment, delivery coordination, and guest relationships without building the whole stack themselves.

## Buyer vs user vs end user

- **Buyer:** restaurant brand leadership, digital teams, operations, and IT
- **Operational users:** marketing, off-premise, payments, and operations teams at restaurant brands
- **End user:** restaurant guests placing orders or interacting with brand communications

## Why the business is interesting

Olo sits in a messy but valuable layer between restaurants and the systems they need to operate modern digital commerce: ordering surfaces, delivery networks, payment processors, POS systems, loyalty tools, and guest-data workflows.

## Known versus inferred

**Known from public sources**
- Olo publicly positions itself around ordering, payments, and guest engagement.
- Olo emphasizes an open platform and a large integration network.
- Olo serves hundreds of restaurant brands and tens of thousands of locations.

**Inferred**
- Olo's strategic value likely increases when a restaurant brand wants to avoid over-dependence on third-party marketplaces while still integrating with them when necessary.
